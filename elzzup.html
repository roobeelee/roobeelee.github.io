Puzzle Hunt Resources

What is a Puzzle Hunt? Read this excellent post first to understand why these resources are useful: https://blog.vero.site/post/puzzlehunts

Nutrimatic Generate words based on possible letter permutations
e.g. search "[abcdef]at" to generate cat, bat, eat, fat; use A for missing letters 		https://nutrimatic.org/
OneLook Generate words based on possible letters (including missing letters)
e.g. search "bl????rd" to find words that start with bl, end with rd, with 4 letters in between 		https://www.onelook.com/
OneLook Reverse Find words based on definitions (can solve crossword style clues & generate list of words based on shared categories) http://onelook.com/reverse-dictionary.shtml
Text Tools Ceaser shifter, word extraction, word search https://tools.qhex.org/
Anagram Solver Generate anagrams (use ? for blank letters) https://www.wordplays.com/anagram-solver/
https://anagram-solver.net/ (larger dictionary)
Alphabet Converter Converts semaphore, Morse code, Braille http://www.npinsker.me/puzzles/monkey/ http://puzzledpint.com/files/2415/7835/9513/CodeSheet-201912.pdf (manual reference)
Reverse Image Search Upload images to search for results on Google https://images.google.com/
More Resources Many more resources available here, but some may take the fun out of puzzling :) https://www.mit.edu/~puzzle/tools.html

Reminders for When You're Stuck

  • Look for hints in the puzzle's title and flavor text
  • Identify where images within the puzzle are from
  • Look for hidden structures or patterns in the puzzle (e.g. bolded words, highlighted areas)
  • Extraction strategies:
    • Take the first letters
    • Index into words
    • Alphabetize the words
    • Sort by word length
    • Find anagrams
    • Diagonalize (take first letter of first word, second letter of second word, etc.)
    • Look for encodings (e.g. binary, ASCII, Morse, Braille, encode into alphabet)
    • Apply an idea from earlier in the puzzle again ("recursion")
